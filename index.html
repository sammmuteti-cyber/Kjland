How long does a land subdivision take?

A straightforward subdivision typically takes 3–6 weeks from field survey to final title registration. We provide a clear timeline estimate at consultation.

What documents do I need to start?

Your original title deed or certificate of lease, national ID or passport, KRA PIN certificate, and any existing survey plans. We review all documents at consultation.

Do you operate outside Nairobi?

Yes — our head office is in Machakos Town. We actively cover Machakos County, Matuu, Nairobi Metro, Mavoko/Athi River, Kitengela, Juja, Ruiru, Ukunda, Diani, Kwale, and extend to all 47 counties with advance notice.

How accurate are your GPS surveys?

Our Trimble RTK GPS achieves ±10mm horizontal and ±20mm vertical accuracy — exceeding the minimum standards set by the Survey Act of Kenya and ISK.

Can you help resolve a boundary dispute?

Yes. We produce certified boundary records admissible in court and at the Land Disputes Tribunal — documenting measurements, methodology, and findings with full legal rigour.
